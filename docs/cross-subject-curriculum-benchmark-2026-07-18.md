# Cross-subject curriculum benchmark — 2026-07-18

## Question

Does giving a fresh subject-generation agent an explicit catalog of established
curricula produce a better physics roadmap than the previous subject-isolated
CLI?

The comparison used:

- before: `/Users/thomasribeiro/notes/subject-benchmarks/2026-07-17-cli-v3/physics`
- after: `/Users/thomasribeiro/notes/subject-benchmarks/2026-07-18-global-curriculum/physics`

The after curriculum was generated by a fresh isolated agent. It received the
standard subject context plus a generated catalog of the existing mathematics
curriculum. The parent CLI then validated mathematics and physics as one graph.

## Quantitative comparison

| Measure | Before | After |
|---|---:|---:|
| Physics decks | 36 | 35 |
| Coverage decisions | 34 | 32 |
| Required edges | 56 | 69 |
| Recommended edges | 47 | 56 |
| Cross-subject required edges | 0 | 13 |
| Cross-subject recommended edges | 0 | 8 |
| Unique external decks reused | 0 | 12 |
| Physics-owned math/programming bridge decks | 4 | 0 |
| Foundational / undergraduate core / undergraduate advanced | 4 / 6 / 16 | 1 / 9 / 14 |
| Graduate / research-specialization | 10 / 0 | 10 / 1 |
| Words across the three curriculum artifacts | 7,207 | 6,916 |

The four duplicated bridge decks removed from physics were
`quantitative-physics-foundations`, `programming-for-physics`,
`mathematical-methods-for-physics`, and `advanced-mathematical-methods`.
They were replaced by precise references to established mathematics
capabilities such as calculus, differential equations, probability,
information theory, differential geometry, and statistical inference.

## What the benchmark exposed

The first cross-subject attempt listed external deck descriptions but omitted
their own prerequisite edges. The agent consequently required both
`mathematics/linear-algebra` and
`mathematics/differential-equations` for one physics deck even though the latter
already guaranteed the former. The parent global validator rejected that
transitively redundant edge. The generated catalog now exposes both required
and recommended dependencies, and the next fresh run passed.

The structurally valid second run still mislabeled four coherent first-graduate
courses as research specializations. A new maturity-transition audit flagged
advanced decks lacking preparation from the immediately preceding learning
level. A fresh refinement then:

- classified quantum information, accelerator physics, quantum field theory,
  and nonequilibrium systems as graduate courses;
- added analytical mechanics as required preparation for accelerator physics;
- added statistical inference as required preparation for the genuinely
  research-facing gravitational-wave route;
- documented the first-chapter capabilities assumed by every graduate and
  research deck.

The final combined graph contains 100 decks across mathematics and physics,
with 13 required and 8 recommended cross-subject links. It has no missing
references, cycles, later-level dependencies, or transitively redundant edges.
Three advisory maturity warnings remain in the pre-existing mathematics
benchmark; none belongs to the newly generated physics curriculum.

## Assessment

The after curriculum is better.

Its main advantage is not that it is shorter. It represents knowledge once,
where it belongs, and reuses that capability through qualified graph edges.
This makes prerequisites inspectable, prevents physics from silently teaching a
second inconsistent mathematics curriculum, and allows later learner guidance
to explain exactly which outside preparation is required versus merely helpful.

The after roadmap also has a more honest progression: introductory physical
reasoning, nine undergraduate-core courses, explicit advanced branches, ten
graduate courses, and one deliberately research-facing route. The old roadmap
had broader prose and one additional deck, but it conflated reusable
mathematical foundations with physics-owned curricula and had no explicit
research-specialization layer.

The benchmark also shows why agent generation cannot be accepted on prose
quality alone. The strongest improvements came from deterministic rejection of
bad graph structure and from an explicit semantic maturity audit followed by a
fresh refinement. Both gates should remain.

This is strong evidence for the architecture, not a universal quality result.
The same before/after trial should be repeated for biology, computer science,
and mathematics before treating the prompt and audit policy as settled.

## Reproduction

```sh
flashcards curriculum validate \
  /Users/thomasribeiro/notes/subject-benchmarks/2026-07-18-global-curriculum

flashcards curriculum audit \
  /Users/thomasribeiro/notes/subject-benchmarks/2026-07-18-global-curriculum

flashcards curriculum build \
  /Users/thomasribeiro/notes/subject-benchmarks/2026-07-18-global-curriculum \
  --output /tmp/global-curriculum.json
```

The built JSON is deterministic for a fixed set of manifests: it does not
include a generation timestamp.
