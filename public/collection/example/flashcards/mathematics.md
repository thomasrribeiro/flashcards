P: How do you solve a quadratic equation $ax^2 + bx + c = 0$?

S:
**IDENTIFY**: Quadratic equation (degree 2 polynomial)

**PLAN**: Use quadratic formula
$$x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}$$

**EXECUTE**:
1. Identify coefficients $a$, $b$, $c$
2. Calculate discriminant: $\Delta = b^2 - 4ac$
3. If $\Delta > 0$: two real solutions
4. If $\Delta = 0$: one repeated solution
5. If $\Delta < 0$: two complex solutions

**EVALUATE**: Check by substituting solutions back into original equation
